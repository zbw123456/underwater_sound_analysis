\section{Executive Summary}
This report presents a complete acoustic analysis of six underwater sound clips extracted from a Baltic Sea recording. The workflow includes:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Time-domain waveform analysis,
    \item Spectrogram-based time--frequency characterization,
    \item Broadband level estimation in \si{\decibel\ re\ micro\pascal},
    \item One-third octave band level analysis,
    \item Feature extraction (RMS, peak, crest factor, dominant frequency, spectral centroid, zero-crossing rate),
    \item Interpretation of likely source characteristics.
\end{itemize}

\section{Methodology}
\subsection{Signal Processing Pipeline}
The recording was scanned for energetic events. The dominant event spans approximately \SIrange{20}{28}{\second}, from which six non-overlapping \SI{1}{\second} clips were extracted for consistent comparison.

\subsection{Broadband Sound Level}
Broadband level is computed as:
\begin{equation}
L_B = 20 \log_{10}\left(\frac{p_{\mathrm{RMS}}}{p_{\mathrm{ref}}}\right),
\end{equation}
where $p_{\mathrm{ref}} = \SI{1}{\micro\pascal}$.

\subsection{One-Third Octave Band Analysis}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Standard center frequencies from \SI{63}{\hertz} to \SI{10000}{\hertz}.
    \item 4th-order Butterworth band-pass filters.
    \item Band levels computed in \si{\decibel\ re\ micro\pascal}.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Spectral Analysis}
\begin{itemize}
    \item STFT spectrograms with logarithmic frequency axis.
    \item Welch PSD estimate (\num{4096}-point FFT).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Extracted Features}
\begin{itemize}
    \item RMS amplitude (V),
    \item Peak amplitude (V),
    \item Crest factor (dimensionless),
    \item Dominant frequency (Hz),
    \item Spectral centroid (Hz),
    \item Zero-crossing rate (dimensionless per frame).
\end{itemize}

\section{Interesting Findings}
\subsection{Consistent Low-Frequency Signature}
A strong and persistent spectral component is observed near \SI{53}{\hertz} in all clips. This repeatability indicates a stable source rather than random impulsive background noise.

\subsection{Moderate and Stable Acoustic Intensity}
Broadband levels remain within a narrow interval (\SIrange{85.2}{87.7}{\decibel\ re\ micro\pascal}), suggesting relatively steady source output over the analyzed window.

\subsection{Energy Concentration at Low Bands}
The one-third octave analysis peaks around \SI{63}{\hertz}, with limited high-frequency dominance. This pattern is typical of low-frequency machinery-like underwater sources.

\subsection{Temporal Behavior}
Crest factors between 2.84 and 3.30 indicate moderate impulsiveness, but not highly transient events. Combined with the stable dominant frequency, this supports a quasi-continuous source process.

\section{Interpretation and Source Hypotheses}
Based on frequency concentration, stability, and level structure, the most plausible source category is low-frequency mechanical activity, such as vessel propulsion or industrial marine equipment. Biological contributions are possible but less consistent with the observed narrow low-frequency dominance and temporal steadiness.
